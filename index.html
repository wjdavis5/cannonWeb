Elect James W Cannon III


To Ohio's 71st District

I am a family man. I have a loving wife, Emily, that I couldn’t be more proud of. She is a successful financial adviser at Rockmill Financial and my strongest supporter. I also have three wonderful children, James, Nicholas, and Victoria. I am an Officer in the Army Reserves and an Afghan veteran. I am a person who is very concerned with the issues facing our community, state, and nation. I feel that jobs are at the top of the list of important issues. Our families deserve the security that gainful employment provides. I believe good jobs and financial security can help us to cope better with other issues facing us. I will work tirelessly to bring prosperity to all our families.Good jobs are the key to a stronger community. Good jobs are the key to a stronger Ohio.

Biography

Biography - Expand

First and foremost, I am a loving husband and proud father of three. I currently serve in the Army Reserves as a Medical Entomologist and Executive Officer of 12th Med Det (Preventive Medicine) . Current Rank: Captain (O-3)

Medical Entomologist
US Army Reserves APMC
November 2007 – Present (6 years 3 months)

Regional Command-West Medical Entomologist (OEF) Afghanistan 2010-2011
Served as the Commander of 787th Med Det PM. Deployed and currently serve as the Executive Officer and Entomologist for 12th Medical Detachment (Preventive Medicine), attached to AMEDD Professional Management Command (APMC).

Duty Description:
Plans, leads, manages, advises, directs, and participates in research, operational and consultative medical entomology, and pest management activities to provide the effective control of pests and vectors of disease affecting health, morale, and environment of all Army personnel.

Additional OEF duty (volunteer):
Assisted JMOC personnel in training Afghan National Army (ANA) preventive medicine (PM) doctors in preventive medicine procedures to include: vector surveillance, potable water testing, DFAC and food inspection, etc. Performed the veterinarian mission in RC-W until Vet assets arrived in our Area of Operations.

Additional Jobs/Offices:
Unit Movement Officer (logistics), Field Ordering Officer, Level 1 Combatives instructor (MACP).

Chemical Officer
US Army Reserves
December 2005 – November 2007 (2 years)Blacklick OH

Served as the Chemical Officer (74D) for 307th Med Grp (now Bde).
Completed CBOLC and the Chemical Defense Training Facility (CDTF).
HAZMAT certified at CBOLC.
Supervised Chemical Specialists and NCO’s.
Served as a Med Platoon Leader and Combatives Instructor (MACP).

Commissioning Source: Officer Candidate School (OCS), Ft Benning, GA

Army Leadership Experience:

    Commanded 787th Med Det: signed for and responsible for unit equipment worth over $1m, as well as for service personnel.


    Responsible for providing Force Health Protection (preventive medicine) to more than 6,000 US/ coalition soldiers and US contractors during OEF.


    Key participant in establishing level 3 Preventive medicine services in RC-W during OEF. We were the 1st PM unit to be assigned to RC-W.


    Served as the Officer in Charge of split operations detachment at Camp Stone and responsible for operations in the Northern provinces in RC-W, as well as Ghor Province


    Unit movement officer (logistics): planned and executed movement of troops and equipment from USA to Afghanistan


    Our preventive medicine measures were crucial to the extremely low disease rate from vector-borne diseases within our area of operations. My vector surveillance plan remained a theater standard for many rotations afterwards.


    Field Ordering Officer: Bartered with local afghan businessmen and vendors for goods and services


    Coordinated with Italian Army in Bala Morghab. Performed courtesy inspection of DFAC. Shared preventive medicine measures that reduced gastrointestinal disease in combat troops.


    Coordinated with Lithuanian Army at FOB Whiskey, Ghor Province. Performed courtesy DFAC and base camp inspection.


    Attended the PM Theater Leadership meeting at Bagram AFB. Being one of the only entomologists to leave large troop centers and travel throughout theater in RC-W, my keen insights led to theater standards of PM Force Health Protection measures and a reassessment of prophylactic use in theater.


    Assisted (volunteer) JMOC to create a continuing education program for ANA officers. Provided Field sanitation and hygiene training for ANA medics. Helped establish the 207th Corps (ANA) Regional Medical PM lab.


    Assisted JMOC in assessing ANSF (afghan security forces) field san/hygiene capabilities at Afghan National Police Ghor Province headquarters and at ANA FOB Bala Morghab. These assessments led ANP Surgeon General to provide additional medical personnel to 606 Zone and expansion of position requirements for ANA PM system.


    In response to an outbreak of gastrointestinal disease among 500 of 1600 ANA soldiers at Camp Zafar I coordinated the provision of potable water reagents and supplies to ANA 207th Corps Regional Medical Center PM dept enabling ANA PM to identify and subsequently eliminate fecal contamination and lack of chlorination of water delivered to Camp Zafar Regional training Center.


    Providing field sanitation reagents also enabled the Regional Support Command- West Commander to appoint the Regional Medical Center PM Dept as a Technical Oversight Representative to the US Military for DFAC, water provision and operating and maintenance contracts exceeding $1.4m in service to Camp Zafar.
